Today’s Quote Process Relies on Manual Coordination Across Teams and Data Sources

Before MQI, sales, sourcing, and operations stitch together the quote by hand across inboxes, spreadsheets, supplier replies, and internal approvals.

02
📥
Customer request arrives

Sales receives drawings, quantity, finish, timing, and commercial context through email and attachments.

→
📊
Inputs are reconstructed

Teams manually gather supplier pricing, labor assumptions, freight, inventory, and timing details from separate sources.

→
🤝
People align on feasibility

Sales, sourcing, and operations trade messages to validate which manufacturer can actually support the order.

→
🧾
Quote is assembled

The final price is built after manual checks, exceptions, and approval handoffs are reconciled.

💸 Margin is rebuilt from supplier cost, labor, freight, and markup inputs while 🏷️ pricing factors shift and ⏱️ data lag from spreadsheets, inboxes, and delayed replies slows quote confidence.

MQI - THE GOAL TO 50% MARGIN - SLIDE 4

Designing the Workflow Before Exposing the Complexity

The operator experiences a clear decision workflow, while a narrow set of specialized agents handles complexity behind the scenes.

04

Operator-facing workflow

1
📥
Order Submitted

Customer files and requirements enter the system.

2
🔍
Requirements Evaluated

Inputs are structured and ambiguity is surfaced early.

3
🏭
Manufacturers Assessed

Viable options are compared on cost, capacity, and timing.

4
🧠
Recommendation Generated

A best-fit path is assembled with rationale and confidence.

5
✅
Human Review

The final decision stays with the operator.

6
📨
Quote Sent

The customer receives a fast, credible response.

Behind the workflow

📥
Intake Agent

Extracts requirements from submitted files.

🏭
Manufacturer Quote Agent

Pulls current supplier pricing.

🧾
Cost Inputs Agent

Adds logistics, lead time, and internal costs.

📈
Margin Optimization Agent

Applies pricing guardrails and commercial logic.

🧠
Decision Layer

Builds the recommendation with rationale.

✅
Human Approver

Sends the quote with accountability.

Design choice

Workflow first for the user. Specialized agents behind the scenes for modularity, trust, and maintainability.

Speaker note: This keeps the architecture, but makes the product flow legible. The audience should understand the workflow first and the agentic system second.

Human Judgment + System Evaluation = Faster, Safer Decisions

The product is designed to support judgment, not replace it: AI narrows uncertainty, while humans remain accountable for the final decision.

05

What the system contributes

  • Recommendation generation
  • Confidence scoring
  • Rationale generation
  • Approval workflow support

What the human retains

  • Judgment over exceptions and tradeoffs
  • Approval authority
  • Commercial accountability
  • Final send decision

Collaboration model

Human judgment + system evaluation = faster, safer decisions with more transparency and less manual reconstruction.

Speaker note: Make the trust model explicit. This is not autonomous quoting. It is decision support designed to improve speed, clarity, and confidence.
